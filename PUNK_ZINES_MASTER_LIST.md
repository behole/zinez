## KEY ARCHIVES & COLLECTIONS

### 1. Internet Archive Punk Zine Collection
- **URL**: https://archive.org/details/misczinespunk
- **Size**: Over 2,000 punk zines
- **Access**: Free digital scans
- **Notable**: Full page scans available

### 2. DC Punk Archive Zine Library
- **Location**: District of Columbia Public Library
- **URL**: https://digdc.dclibrary.org/
- **Period**: Mid-1970s to present (majority from 1980s)
- **Notable**: Virtual collection from DC Punk Archive donors
- **Content**: Interviews, concert reviews, record reviews, photos

### 3. Flickr 1970s Fanzines Collection
- **URL**: https://www.flickr.com/photos/stillunusual/albums/72157657899141379/
- **Content**: Front cover scans of 1970s punk fanzines
- **Notable**: Personal collection arranged chronologically

### 4. Digital Fanzine Preservation Society (DFPS)
- **Period**: 2009-2011
- **Content**: Remastered hardcore and punk music fanzines
- **Format**: Blog-based archive

## ICONIC PUNK ZINES (1970s-1980s)

### SNIFFIN' GLUE (UK, 1976-1977)
- **Founded**: July 13, 1976
- **Founder**: Mark Perry
- **Issues**: 12 total
- **Circulation**: Started at 50, reached 15,000
- **Named After**: Ramones song "Now I Wanna Sniff Some Glue"
- **Notable Features**:
  - First British punk fanzine
  - DIY aesthetic with typewriter and felt-tip pen
  - Final issue included flexi disc of Alternative TV
  - MoMA Collection piece (1978 compilation)
- **Visual Style**: 
  - Photocopied pages
  - Hand-scrawled lettering
  - Cut-and-paste aesthetic
  - Rub-down letterset

### PUNK MAGAZINE (USA, 1976-1979)
- **Founded**: January 1976
- **Location**: New York City
- **Founders**: John Holmstrom, Ged Dunn, Legs McNeil
- **Notable**: First to popularize the term "punk rock"
- **First Issue Cover**: Lou Reed

### MAXIMUM ROCKNROLL (USA, 1982-2019, online 2019-present)
- **Location**: San Francisco/Berkeley
- **Founder**: Tim Yohannan
- **Circulation**: 16,000 print run
- **Format**: Monthly
- **Notable**: Most influential US hardcore zine
- **Content**: Music, politics, scene reports

### FLIPSIDE (USA, 1977-2000)
- **Location**: Los Angeles
- **Notable Issues**: 
  - Issue #24 (1980): Jack Grisham/TSOL cover
- **Content**: Interviews, reviews, scene coverage

### SLASH (USA, 1977-1980)
- **Location**: Los Angeles
- **Notable**: Covered early LA punk scene

### SEARCH & DESTROY (USA, 1977)
- **Location**: San Francisco
- **Later became**: RE/Search

### RIPPED & TORN (UK, 1976-1979)
- **Notable**: "Loudest Punk Fanzine in the UK"
- **Content**: Early punk scene documentation

## VISUAL CHARACTERISTICS

### Common Design Elements:
- **Typography**: 
  - Typewriter text
  - Hand-lettered titles
  - Rub-down letters (Letraset)
  - Felt-tip pen writing

- **Layout**:
  - Cut-and-paste collage
  - Photocopied pages
  - DIY aesthetic
  - Haphazard arrangement

- **Imagery**:
  - Band photos
  - Concert flyers
  - Hand-drawn illustrations
  - Xeroxed artwork

- **Production**:
  - Black and white predominant
  - Some later issues with spot color
  - Stapled binding
  - Various paper sizes
